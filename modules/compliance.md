# 📋 Module 6: Compliance & Certification Guard

## Based On
B Corp certification standards, organic certification standards (general), Fair Trade certification, regulatory compliance frameworks, ISO standards, audit requirements.

## Core Question

> Does this decision risk any existing certification, breach any regulation, or create unacceptable legal/compliance exposure?

## Evaluation Criteria

### Criteria 1: Certification Risk
- **0–25**: Decision would likely violate certification requirements (e.g., B Corp, Fair Trade, organic)
- **26–50**: Decision creates material risk of non-compliance with certification standards
- **51–75**: Decision is compliant but with minor concerns to monitor
- **76–100**: Decision is fully compliant with all active certifications and standards

### Criteria 2: Legal & Regulatory Compliance
- **0–25**: Decision violates laws or regulations
- **26–50**: Decision tests legal boundaries or operates in grey areas
- **51–75**: Decision is compliant with applicable law
- **76–100**: Decision exceeds minimum legal requirements in alignment with best practice

### Criteria 3: Audit Readiness
- **0–25**: No documentation trail would survive an audit
- **26–50**: Incomplete documentation, gaps in evidence
- **51–75**: Adequate documentation for standard audit
- **76–100**: Exemplary documentation — audit would confirm best practice

### Criteria 4: Supply Chain/Traceability
- **0–25**: No supply chain visibility or due diligence
- **26–50**: Partial visibility with gaps
- **51–75**: Reasonable supply chain monitoring
- **76–100**: Full traceability with documented ethical sourcing

### Criteria 5: Timing & Reporting Obligations
- **0–25**: Decision creates reporting failures or deadline breaches
- **26–50**: Timing will be tight with some reporting risk
- **51–75**: Adequate time for all reporting obligations
- **76–100**: Decision enhances reporting capability or creates buffer

## Questions to Ask

- Does this action risk any existing certification the business holds (B Corp, fair trade, organic, etc.)?
- Are there regulatory or legal requirements being met?
- Is there an adequate audit trail?
- Would this pass a certification or regulatory audit?
- Are there timing implications (cert renewals, reporting deadlines, tax obligations)?
- What is the worst-case regulatory scenario if this decision goes wrong?
- If this decision was investigated, would the documentation stand up?
- Are there any whistleblower risks or obligations?

## Certifications Reference

When evaluating, check against the relevant certifications for the context:

| Certification | Key Requirements |
|---------------|-----------------|
| B Corp | Governance, Workers, Community, Environment, Customers — 80+ BIA score |
| Fair Trade | Fair prices, decent working conditions, local sustainability, transparency |
| Organic (general) | No synthetic inputs, soil health, biodiversity, full traceability |
| FSC (Forestry) | Responsible forest management, chain of custody |
| ISO 14001 | Environmental management system, continuous improvement |
| SA8000 | Social accountability, workers' rights, no forced labour |
| B-Corp Recertification | Every 3 years — material changes must be reported |

## Edge Cases

- **Barely legal**: Compliance with the letter but not the spirit of regulation scores amber, not green. The filter checks intent, not just legality.
- **Jurisdictional shopping**: Choosing to operate in a jurisdiction specifically to avoid stricter regulation is a red flag. Score down accordingly.
- **Proactive vs. reactive compliance**: A decision that proactively exceeds compliance standards scores higher than one that minimally complies.
- **Cross-border complexity**: When multiple regulatory regimes apply, the strictest standard should be the baseline.
- **Emerging regulation**: If regulation is expected (e.g., upcoming climate disclosure laws), a forward-looking decision accounts for this even if not yet mandatory.

## Flag Thresholds

- 🟢 Score ≥ 70: Proceed — compliance is solid
- 🟡 Score 40–69: Flag — compliance gaps need attention before proceeding
- 🔴 Score < 40: Block — legal or certification risk is unacceptable
