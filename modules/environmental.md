# 🌏 Module 1: Environmental Stewardship

## Based On
B Corp Environment criteria, ESG frameworks, Triple Bottom Line (People/Planet/Profit), IFOAM Ecology principle, circular economy principles.

## Core Question

> What is the impact of this decision on the natural world, and is there a more environmentally sound alternative?

## Evaluation Criteria

Each criterion is scored 0–100. The module score is the **average** of all applicable criteria.

### Criteria 1: Direct Environmental Impact
- **0–25**: Severe, irreversible damage (toxic contamination, deforestation, habitat destruction)
- **26–50**: Significant negative impact (high emissions, excessive waste, water depletion)
- **51–75**: Minor negative impact with mitigation measures
- **76–100**: Neutral or positive environmental contribution

### Criteria 2: Lifecycle Thinking
- **0–25**: No consideration of full lifecycle (source → use → disposal)
- **26–50**: Partial consideration with gaps
- **51–75**: Reasonable lifecycle assessment considered
- **76–100**: Full circularity — designed for regeneration, reuse, or minimal end-of-life impact

### Criteria 3: Resource Efficiency
- **0–25**: Excessive resource use, planned obsolescence, single-use
- **26–50**: Standard efficiency with room for improvement
- **51–75**: Above-average efficiency, use of renewables
- **76–100**: Leading efficiency, regenerative resource loops, zero waste

### Criteria 4: Biodiversity & Ecosystem Health
- **0–25**: Active harm to biodiversity and ecosystems
- **26–50**: Neglects biodiversity considerations
- **51–75**: Considers biodiversity with mitigation
- **76–100**: Actively enhances biodiversity and ecosystem health

## Questions to Ask

- What is the carbon / waste / water impact of this decision?
- Is there a more environmentally sound alternative?
- Does this support circularity (reduce, reuse, recycle, regenerate)?
- Is packaging / transport / sourcing minimised?
- Does it avoid harm to biodiversity and ecosystems?
- Does it consider the full lifecycle from source to end-of-life?
- What happens to this decision's outputs in 1 year? 10 years? 100 years?
- Is there greenwashing risk — does this appear better than it actually is?

## Edge Cases

- **Trade-offs**: A decision that harms environment locally but helps globally (e.g., manufacturing solar panels). Resolution: Score both impacts separately, weigh net effect.
- **Inaction**: Sometimes NOT acting is the most environmentally harmful choice (e.g., not replacing a leaky system). Factor in the status quo baseline.
- **Offsetting**: Carbon offsets count only if verified and additional. Offsets are not a substitute for reduction.
- **Relative improvement**: A change from "terrible" to "bad" is progress but still scores low on absolute terms. Score both relative and absolute.

## Flag Thresholds

- 🟢 Score ≥ 70: Proceed
- 🟡 Score 40–69: Flag for review — are the environmental costs justified?
- 🔴 Score < 40: Block — environmental impact is unacceptable at current state
