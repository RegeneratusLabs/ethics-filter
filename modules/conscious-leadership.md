# 🧘 Module 4: Conscious Leadership & Values Alignment

## Based On
Conscious Leadership 15 Commitments (Jim Dethmer, Diana Chapman, Kaley Klemp), Conscious Capitalism (Mackey & Sisodia), Values-Based Leadership (Harry Kraemer), F-A-T-H-E-R framework.

## Core Question

> Does this decision come from above the line (curiosity, commitment, ownership) or below the line (defensiveness, blame, denial)? Does it align with the deepest values of those involved?

## Key Framework: Above/Below the Line

```
Above the Line (Conscious):
- Open / curious / learning
- Committed to outcomes
- Taking ownership / accountability
- Focused on what can be controlled
- "What can I learn?"
- Generous interpretation of others

Below the Line (Unconscious):
- Closed / defensive / certain
- Committed to being right
- Blaming others / victim mentality
- Focused on what can't be controlled
- "Whose fault is this?"
- Negative interpretation of others
```

## Evaluation Criteria

### Criteria 1: Above/Below the Line Assessment
- **0–25**: Decision clearly comes from below the line (fear, control, blame, avoidance)
- **26–50**: Mixed — some below-the-line dynamics present
- **51–75**: Predominantly above the line with minor concerns
- **76–100**: Clear above-the-line decision-making — ownership, curiosity, commitment

### Criteria 2: Values Alignment
- **0–25**: Decision contradicts the stated values of the individual/organization
- **26–50**: Values not explicitly considered in the decision
- **51–75**: Values considered and mostly honoured
- **76–100**: Decision directly embodies and demonstrates core values

### Criteria 3: Long-Term vs Short-Term Orientation
- **0–25**: Sacrifices long-term integrity for short-term gain
- **26–50**: Short-term focus with some long-term consideration
- **51–75**: Balanced short and long-term thinking
- **76–100**: Decisions guided by long-term vision, patient capital thinking

### Criteria 4: Personal Growth Orientation
- **0–25**: Defensive — refuses feedback, avoids learning, blames others
- **26–50**: Open to feedback but doesn't act on it
- **51–75**: Actively seeks feedback and adjusts
- **76–100**: Decision framed as learning opportunity regardless of outcome

## Questions to Ask

- Does this decision come from above the line (curiosity, commitment, ownership) or below the line (defensiveness, blame, denial)?
- Does it align with the stated core values?
- Does it serve the long-term vision or just short-term convenience?
- Is there a more creative, values-aligned alternative?
- Would this decision be made if nobody was watching?
- Does this reflect the kind of person or organization the decision-maker truly wants to be?
- What is the motivation behind this decision — is it clean or is there shadow motivation?
- If this decision fails, will the response be learning or blame?

## Edge Cases

- **"Good" outcome from a bad process**: Even if a decision works out, if it came from below the line (fear, control, manipulation), it's still a red flag. Process matters as much as outcome.
- **Organizational vs. personal values**: An individual may have to make a decision that serves their organization but conflicts with personal values. This is a tension that must be flagged, not resolved by ignoring one set of values.
- **Cultural leadership models**: "Above the line" principles are universal (ownership, openness, learning) but may be expressed differently across cultures. Respect cultural context while holding the principles.
- **The "good leader" blind spot**: Arrogance about one's own ethical leadership can prevent genuine self-reflection. The filter should be humble — always open to being wrong.

## Flag Thresholds

- 🟢 Score ≥ 70: Proceed — conscious, values-aligned
- 🟡 Score 40–69: Flag — explore the motivation and alignment more deeply
- 🔴 Score < 40: Block — below-the-line dynamics likely to cause harm or regret
