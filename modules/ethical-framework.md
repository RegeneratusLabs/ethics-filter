# ⚖️ Module 5: Ethical Decision Framework

## Based On
Markkula Center for Applied Ethics (Santa Clara University) 5-Step Framework, Michael Davis 7-Step Method, IDEA Framework (Trillium Health Partners), IFOAM Care principle.

## Core Question

> What do different ethical traditions say about this decision, and can I defend this choice using rigorous ethical reasoning?

## The Process

This module runs the decision through **five ethical lenses** in sequence, then synthesises the results.

### Lens 1: Utilitarian — Greatest Good
Ask: *Which option produces the greatest good for the greatest number of people?*

- Consider all stakeholders
- Weigh benefits vs. harms
- Avoid the tyranny of the majority — minority harms still matter
- **Score 0–100**: How well does the decision maximize net good?

### Lens 2: Rights — Dignity & Autonomy
Ask: *Does this decision respect the fundamental rights and dignity of all involved?*

- Human rights (UN Declaration)
- Right to informed consent
- Right to privacy and autonomy
- Right to fair process
- **Score 0–100**: How well are rights respected?

### Lens 3: Fairness / Justice — Equity & Desert
Ask: *Is this decision fair, equitable, and just? Does it treat similar cases similarly?*

- Distributive justice (are benefits and burdens fairly shared?)
- Procedural justice (was the process fair?)
- Corrective justice (if harm occurs, is there redress?)
- **Score 0–100**: How just is this decision?

### Lens 4: Common Good — Community & Belonging
Ask: *Does this decision contribute to the common good of the community?*

- Does it strengthen social bonds?
- Does it support shared resources and institutions?
- Does it consider the least advantaged?
- **Score 0–100**: How well does this serve the common good?

### Lens 5: Virtue — Character & Integrity
Ask: *What would a person of strong moral character do in this situation?*

- Honesty, compassion, courage, integrity, humility
- Would this decision make you proud?
- Is there a gap between who you are and who you want to be?
- **Score 0–100**: How virtuous is this decision?

**Module Score**: Average of all five lenses.

**Tensions**: Note any lenses that conflict (e.g., Utilitarian says yes, Rights says no). Flag these as ethical tensions that require human judgment.

## Final Questions

- Can I defend this choice to a committee of peers?
- Would I advise someone I love to make this same decision?
- Is there any ethical perspective I'm avoiding or dismissing?
- What would the most ethical person I know do?

## Edge Cases

- **Ethical pluralism**: Different ethical traditions can reach different conclusions. This module doesn't pick a "winner" — it surfaces the tensions for human judgment.
- **Moral luck**: Don't judge decisions purely by outcomes. A well-reasoned decision that has a bad outcome is still ethically sound. A poorly reasoned decision that works out is still ethically flawed.
- **Self-deception**: The most dangerous ethical failures are the ones we rationalise. If the reasoning feels too convenient, check again.
- **Everyone does it**: Not a valid ethical argument. Each decision stands on its own merits.
- **The precautionary principle (IFOAM Care)**: Where there is risk of serious or irreversible harm, lack of full scientific certainty is not a reason to postpone action to prevent harm. Score down decisions that ignore precaution.

## Flag Thresholds

- 🟢 Score ≥ 70: Proceed — ethically sound across multiple lenses
- 🟡 Score 40–69: Flag — ethical tensions exist, explore further
- 🔴 Score < 40: Block — significant ethical concerns from multiple lenses
